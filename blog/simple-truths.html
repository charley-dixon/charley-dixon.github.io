Simple Truths Learned in the Wilderness

The first time I went mountaineering I received a wake up call from reality thirty seconds into the initial ascent. Keeled onto my knees, I felt as though I was sucking air through lungs pinched halfway shut. A pang of anxiety shot down my spine as I realized I was standing two thousand three hundred feet below the twelve thousand five hundred foot summit of West Maroon Pass, and turning around was not an option. As my survival instincts kicked in my mind began to view the world around me with clarity.

Despite the fear described above, I have learned to love these types of situations because life reveals simple truths to a clear mind. The following is an ever-growing list of truths learned so far:

  • A great challenge is an even greater learning opportunity
  • Inner discovery begins with surrendering to the outer world
  • Pain is transmutable energy
  • The only actions you can control exist in the present
  • Traditions make for good times, great memories, and stronger friendships
